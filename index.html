About blood donation

Why your donation matters

Blood cannot be manufactured. Every unit transfused, whether for a mother bleeding after childbirth, a child with severe malaria-related anaemia, an accident victim, or a person with sickle cell disease, comes from someone who chose to give. Hospitals need a steady, willing supply every single day, not only during emergencies.

Benefits of donating
  • Free mini health checkEvery visit includes a check of your haemoglobin, blood pressure, pulse and weight before you donate.
  • Lowers excess iron storesRegular donation helps reduce excess iron in the blood, which over time supports healthy blood vessels.
  • Refreshes your blood supplyYour body replaces the donated volume with new blood cells, a natural renewal process.
  • A direct, visible act of goodDonated blood is typically used within the same hospital network within days, often for someone you'll never meet.
Who can donate
  • Age 18 to 60 yearsDonors should be between 18 and 60 years old and generally in good health.
  • Weight of 50kg or moreThis ensures the donation volume is safe relative to your total blood volume.
  • Generally healthy on the dayNo fever, cold, recent major surgery, or current pregnancy/breastfeeding.

Before you come
  • Eat a proper meal within 3 hours before donating — don't come on an empty stomach.
  • Drink plenty of water the night before and the morning of your visit.
  • Rest well before coming.
  • Come a little early to complete screening without feeling rushed.
  • Wear clothing with sleeves that roll up easily.
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before your appointment.
